\section{Systematics}
\label{sec:monoZ-syst}

Systematic uncertainties in the search include both theoretical and experimental sources.

For theoretical modelling uncertainties, the analysis follows as closely as possible the ATLAS Physics Modelling Group (PMG) recommendations and prescriptions. The main components are listed below:
\begin{itemize}

\item The effect of renormalisation and factorisation scale variations ($\mu_R, \mu_F$) is evaluated using a three-point variation scheme, in which $\mu_R$ and $\mu_F$ are scaled simultaneously by factors of 2 and 1/2 to obtain the upward and downward variations, respectively. In general, both signal and background simulated samples provide internal event weights that are used to estimate these variations.
The QCD scale uncertainties range from 4 - 5\% in different signal samples.

\item Parton-shower and hadronisation uncertainties are evaluated using Eigentune variations of the A14 tune, where available (e.g., for top-pair production). For signal and diboson samples generated with Sherpa, alternative truth-level samples are used.
Typical parton-shower uncertainties range from 1 - 4\% for different MC samples.

\item For the signal and $ZZ$ samples, dedicated corrections are applied to account for electroweak (EWK) effects, as described in the corresponding sections. The uncertainty of the EWK correction is about 2\%.

\item For top-quark processes, alternative samples are used to evaluate the effects of different MC generators on the hard-scattering process (e.g., POWHEG vs.\ aMC@NLO) and on the parton-showering model (e.g., POWHEG+PYTHIA vs.\ POWHEG+HERWIG7). The uncertainty is at a 1\% level.

\item The impact of parton distribution function (PDF) uncertainties is evaluated for both signal and background samples. The uncertainties range from 1 - 2\%.

\end{itemize}

The experimental uncertainties include contributions from lepton reconstruction and identification, trigger efficiencies, and the energy/momentum scale and resolution. Systematic uncertainties related to jets, $E_\mathrm{T}^\mathrm{miss}$, flavor tagging, pile-up, and luminosity are also considered. The impact of each major experimental systematic on the signal is reported as "up" ($+1\sigma$) and "down" ($-1\sigma$) variations. Overall, the total experimental uncertainties remain below 2.5\% for all signal samples.

For some non-dominant backgrounds in the control regions (CRs), systematic uncertainties are assumed to be the same as those in the signal region (SR). Specifically, uncertainties on $Z$+jets are set to 38\% in the $e\mu$ and $3\ell$ CRs, while $WZ$ uncertainties in the $e\mu$ region are set to 9\%. The $ZZ \to \ell\ell\nu\nu$ uncertainties in the $e\mu$ and $3\ell$ CRs are set to 6.6\%, and 8.8\% for $ZZ \to 4\ell$. uncertainties of the non-resonant processes in the $3\ell$ CR are set to 14\%. For triboson and $t\bar{t}V$ processes, uncertainties of 50\% and 20\% are assumed, respectively.
