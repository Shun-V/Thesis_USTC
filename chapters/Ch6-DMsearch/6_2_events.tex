\section{Event Selection}
\label{sec:monoZ_selection}

The analysis targets events with a leptonic $Z$ boson candidate and large missing transverse momentum. The selection is optimized to maximize the significance of DM signals against the SM background, primarily diboson and $Z$+jets processes.

%\subsection{Object Selection}
%\label{subsec:object_sel}

Candidate events are required to have a primary vertex with at least two associated tracks with $p_{\mathrm{T}} > 1$~GeV. The physics objects used in this analysis, electrons, muons, jets, and missing transverse momentum, are defined as described in Chapter~\ref{chap:zz}.

For the final signal selection, electrons must satisfy "Medium" likelihood-based identification criteria and "FixedCutPflowLoose" isolation requirements.

\subsection{Event Pre-selection}
\label{subsec:pre_selection}

Events are selected using \texttt{single-electron} or \texttt{single-muon} triggers~\cite{ATLAS:2020ejn}. The baseline pre-selection criteria are designed to identify a $Z$ boson candidate while ensuring high efficiency:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Leptons:} Exactly two same-flavor, opposite-sign (SFOS) leptons ($\ell = e, \mu$) are required. The leading lepton must have $p_{\mathrm{T}} > 30$ GeV, and the sub-leading lepton must have $p_{\mathrm{T}} > 20$ GeV.
    \item \textbf{Z-mass window:} The invariant mass of the lepton pair must fall within $76 < m_{\ell\ell} < 106$ GeV, consistent with the $Z$ boson mass to suppress non-resonant dilepton backgrounds.
    \item \textbf{Third lepton veto:} Events containing a third lepton with $p_{\mathrm{T}} > 7$ GeV (satisfying "Loose" identification) are vetoed to suppress the $WZ$ background.
    \item \textbf{$b$-jet veto:} Events containing any $b$-tagged jets ($p_{\mathrm{T}} > 20$ GeV, $|\eta| < 2.5$) are vetoed to suppress top-quark backgrounds ($t\bar{t}$, $Wt$). The \texttt{MV2c10} tagger~\cite{ATLAS:2019bwq} is used at the 85\% efficiency working point.
    \item \textbf{$E_{\mathrm{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ threshold:} A relatevily loose requirement of $E_{\mathrm{T}}^{\text{miss}} > 70$ GeV is applied at the pre-selection stage.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Signal Region Definition}
\label{subsec:sr_def}

To further enhance the sensitivity to Dark Matter signals, specific kinematic requirements are applied to define the Signal Region (SR), presented in the first column of Table~\ref{tab:SR_CR_selection}.
The optimization was further performed using an iterative BDT approach and the signal significance calculations:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{$E_{\mathrm{T}}^{\text{miss}} > 90$ GeV:} A tighter $E_{\mathrm{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ cut is applied to reduce the $Z+\text{jets}$ background, where $E_{\mathrm{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ often arises from instrumental mis-measurement.
    \item \textbf{$E_{\mathrm{T}}^{\text{miss}}$-significance $> 9$:} The object-based $E_{\mathrm{T}}^{\text{miss}}$-significance ($S$) is a powerful discriminant against fake missing energy. It assesses whether the observed $E_{\mathrm{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ is consistent with the resolution of the reconstructed objects. A cut of $S > 9$ significantly suppresses the $Z+\text{jets}$ contribution. 
    
    \item \textbf{$\Delta R_{\ell\ell} < 1.8$:} The angular separation between the two leptons is required to be small. This selects events where the $Z$ boson is boosted, recoiling against the invisible DM system. 
\end{itemize}

The event selection is to optimize the signal significance ($Z$), which is defined as~\cite{ATL-PHYS-PUB-2020-025}:
    \begin{equation}
        Z = \sqrt{2\left((s+b)\ln\left[\frac{(s+b)(b+\sigma_{b}^{2})}{b^{2}+(s+b)\sigma_{b}^{2}}\right] - \frac{b^{2}}{\sigma_{b}^{2}}\ln\left[1+\frac{s\sigma_{b}^{2}}{b(b+\sigma_{b}^{2})}\right]\right)}
    \end{equation}

For the final statistical interpretation of the Mono-$Z$ channel, the transverse mass of the $ZZ$ system, $m_\text{T}^\text{ZZ}$, is used as the discriminant variable. It is defined as:
\begin{equation}
    m_\text{T}^\text{ZZ} = \sqrt{\left(\sqrt{m_{Z}^{2} + |p_{T}^{\ell\ell}|^{2}} + \sqrt{m_{Z}^{2} + |E_\text{T}^{\text{miss}}|^{2}}\right)^{2} - \left|\vec{p}_{T}^{\,\ell\ell} + \vec{E}_\text{T}^{\text{miss}}\right|^{2}}
\end{equation}
This variable effectively separates the signal (which peaks at higher values correlated with the mediator mass) from the SM backgrounds. For the Mono-Z search, only events with $m_\text{T}^\text{ZZ} > 200$ GeV are included in the fit. 

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
Considering all Mono-Z signal events predicted from different models, in which the $Z$ boson decays into $e^+e^-$ or $\mu^+\mu^-$ pair, the SR selection has an acceptance times efficiency of about $8\%$ ($19\%$) for quark-induced (gluon-induced) $ZH \to \ell^+\ell^- + \mathrm{inv}$ production, resulting in an expectation of 120 events for $\mathcal{B}(H \to \mathrm{inv}) = 10\%$. 

For an example signal point in the simplified DM model ($m_{\chi} = 1$~GeV, $m_{\mathrm{med}} = 900$~GeV), the acceptance times efficiency is about $20\%$, corresponding to 145 expected events. For a $gg$-induced 2HDM+$a$ signal point ($\tan\beta = 1.0$, $\sin\theta = 0.7$, $m_{A} = 600$~GeV, $m_{a} = 400$~GeV, $m_{\chi} = 10$~GeV), it is about $32\%$, corresponding to 182 expected events. 

For all signal models, the acceptance times efficiency is very similar for the dielectron and dimuon selections.
