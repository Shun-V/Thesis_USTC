\subsection{Background Selection} % 删除了多余的空格
\label{sec:monoZ_bkg}

To accurately constrain the normalization factors of the primary background processes contributing to the signal region (SR), a set of three specific control regions (CRs) is established. The exact kinematic definitions governing these CRs are outlined below.

\paragraph{The $e\mu$ CR:}
Constructed with kinematic criteria identical to the SR, this phase space diverges only by mandating an opposite-flavor lepton pair ($e\mu$). Its primary function is to anchor the normalizations of non-resonant processes (namely $t\bar{t}$, single top-quark, $\text{WW}$, and $\text{Z} \to \tau\tau$). The resulting selection yields a non-resonant background purity of approximately 98\%.

\paragraph{The $3\ell$ CR:}
Dedicated to the calibration of the $\text{WZ}$ diboson estimate, this region enforces an exact three-lepton requirement. A valid $\text{Z}$ boson candidate is reconstructed from two same-flavor, opposite-charge leptons satisfying $p_{\text{T}}^{\ell} > 20,\,30~\text{GeV}$ within an invariant mass window of $76 < m_{\ell\ell} < 106~\text{GeV}$. A third isolated lepton possessing $p_{\text{T}}^{\ell} > 20~\text{GeV}$ is further required. To suppress contamination, thresholds of $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}} > 30~\text{GeV}$ and $S_{E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}} > 3$ are enforced. Furthermore, the transverse mass associated with the $\text{W}$ boson candidate must satisfy the condition:
\begin{equation}
m_{\text{T}}^{\text{W}} =
\sqrt{
2\,p_{\text{T}}^{\ell}\,
E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}\,
\left(1 - \cos\Delta\phi(\ell, E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}})\right)
}
> 60~\text{GeV},
\end{equation}
where $\Delta\phi(\ell, E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}})$ defines the azimuthal separation between the third lepton and the missing transverse momentum vector. This carefully sculpted $3\ell$ CR achieves a $\text{WZ}$ process purity of roughly 93\%.

\paragraph{The $4\ell$ CR:}
To effectively evaluate the irreducible $\text{ZZ}$ background, this CR mandates the presence of exactly four reconstructed leptons. The event must feature two pairs of same-flavor, oppositely charged leptons, which, when sorted by ascending transverse momentum, satisfy $p_{\text{T}}^{\ell} > 7,\,15,\,15,\,27~\text{GeV}$. In scenarios where all four constituent leptons share an identical flavor, the combinatorial ambiguity is resolved by selecting the pairing configuration that minimizes the metric $|m_{\ell\ell_1} - m_{\text{Z}}| + |m_{\ell\ell_2} - m_{\text{Z}}|$, setting $m_{\text{Z}} = 91.19~\text{GeV}$. Both identified lepton pairs are strictly required to fall within the $76 < m_{\ell\ell} < 106~\text{GeV}$ invariant mass window.

To replicate the missing energy topology inherent to the SR, one lepton pair (selected at random) is artificially treated as invisible particles, enabling the calculation of proxy variables ${E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}}'$ and $S_{{E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}}'}$. Subsequently, the SR baseline criteria are imposed upon these pseudo-variables: ${E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}}' > 90~\text{GeV}$ and $S_{{E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}}'} > 9$, while the remaining visible lepton pair must satisfy $\Delta R_{\ell\ell} < 1.8$. This procedural approach ensures the $4\ell$ CR remains nearly 100\% pure in genuine $\text{ZZ}$ events.

Within the SR, the $\text{ZZ}$ continuum acts as the most prominent background, closely followed by $\text{WZ}$, $\text{Z}$+jets, and the aggregated non-resonant sources (comprising $\text{WW}$, $t\bar{t}$, single top-quark, and $\text{Z} \to \tau\tau$). Minor contaminations originating from the $\text{VVV}$, $t\bar{t}\text{V}$, and $\text{ZZ} \to 4\ell$ processes are also accounted for.

\section{Improving the Signal Sensitivity with a BDT}

To maximize the discovery potential for the $\text{H} \to \text{invisible}$ process, a multivariate approach leveraging a Boosted Decision Tree (BDT) is deployed via the TMVA framework~\cite{Hoecker:2007tmva}, significantly enhancing the discrimination capabilities against SM backgrounds. By simultaneously processing multiple weakly discriminating kinematic features, the BDT constructs a single highly sensitive discriminant, thereby delivering a classification performance substantially superior to that of any individual variable.

A set of eight distinctive input variables is incorporated: $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}/H_{\text{T}}$, $S_{E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}}$, $H_{\text{T}}$, the soft-term fraction $f_{\text{soft}} = |1 - |\vec{E}_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}} + \vec{p}_{\text{T}}^{\text{jets}}| / p_{\text{T}}^{\ell\ell}|$, $m_{\ell\ell}$, $\Delta R_{\ell\ell}$, the rapidity of the dilepton system ($Y_{\ell\ell}$), and the azimuthal separation between the dilepton transverse momentum and the missing energy ($\Delta\phi(\ell\ell, E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}})$). Although supplementary variables were initially investigated, they yielded negligible gains in performance and were subsequently discarded.

Training of the BDT is executed exclusively within the signal region (SR) phase space, utilizing simulated $\text{ZH} \to \ell\ell + \text{invisible}$ events as the signal model against a blended dataset of background samples acquired from the CR selections. The continuous output score generated by this BDT, illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:bdt}(a), serves as the primary observable for the profile likelihood extraction within the SR and the $e\mu$ CR (depicted in Figure~\ref{fig:bdt}(b)). Conversely, within the $3\ell$ and $4\ell$ CRs, the fit strictly relies on the $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ spectrum, given that several fundamental BDT input variables display pronounced shape discrepancies between these CRs and the SR.
