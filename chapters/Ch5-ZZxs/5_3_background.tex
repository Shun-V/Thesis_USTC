\section{Background Estimation}
\label{sec:llvv_background}

Evaluating the $\text{ZZ} \to \ell^+\ell^-\nu\bar{\nu}$ cross-section requires strictly accounting for multiple interfering processes. These contaminating sources are broadly separated into two primary classes:

\begin{itemize}

    % --- FIRST TOP-LEVEL ITEM ---
    \item \textbf{Inseparable (Irreducible) Backgrounds}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Diboson processes:}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item $\text{WW} \to \ell\nu\ell\nu$: The escaping neutrinos generate authentic $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$, perfectly mimicking the targeted $\text{ZZ} \to \ell\ell\nu\nu$ topology.
            \item $\text{WZ} \to \ell\nu\ell\ell$: Should the detector hardware fail to register one lepton originating from the $\text{Z}$ boson, this interaction directly mirrors the signal state.
            \item $\text{ZZ} \to \ell\ell\ell\ell$: Acts as baseline background contamination whenever two constituent leptons successfully evade detection.
        \end{itemize}
    \end{itemize}

    % --- SECOND TOP-LEVEL ITEM ---
    \item \textbf{Mitigable (Reducible) Backgrounds}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Top-quark backgrounds:}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item $t\bar{t}$: If $b$-jets embedded within $t\bar{t}$ decay chains escape proper tagging, the event can artificially replicate the signal.
            \item Single top ($\text{W}t$): Introduces a contamination mechanism functionally identical to standard $t\bar{t}$ events.
        \end{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Drell--Yan + jets} ($\text{Z} \to \ell\ell$ + jets): Flawed jet energy readings artificially manufacture a fake $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ signature.
        \item \textbf{$\text{W}$ + jets}: Single $\text{W}$ bosons undergoing leptonic decay, when paired with accompanying jets, can spoof the signal region if a jet is erroneously classified as a valid lepton.
    \end{itemize}

\end{itemize}

Securing an exact cross-section measurement demands rigorous background quantification via specialized control regions (CRs). Dictated by their distinct final-state topologies, these interfering processes are routed into the subsequent CR brackets:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Non-Resonant Backgrounds} (sourced from $t\bar{t}, \text{W}t, \text{WW}, \tau\tau$):\\
    Because these interactions decay via paired $\text{W}$ bosons (or $\tau$ leptons) rather than a singular $\text{Z}$ boson, they inherently yield uncorrelated lepton flavors. To effectively decouple Top processes from $\text{WW}$ events, they are further partitioned according to $b$-jet multiplicity:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{0 $b$-jet Category ($\text{WW}$ Enriched):}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item $\text{WW} \to \ell\nu\ell\nu$: Operates as the prevailing background within the zero $b$-tag domain, generating authentic $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ via neutrinos.
            \item $t\bar{t}$ / Single Top ($\text{W}t$): Top-quark events only leak into this bracket if their constituent $b$-jets elude the tagging algorithm or drop outside physical detector acceptance.
        \end{itemize}
        \item \textbf{$\ge 1$ $b$-jet Category (Top Enriched):}
        \begin{itemize}
             \item $t\bar{t} \to \text{W}b\text{W}b$ and $\text{W}t$: Represent the dominant interference once at least one $b$-jet is positively identified, bringing real $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ supplied by decaying $\text{W}$ bosons.
        \end{itemize}
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{3-Lepton Backgrounds ($\text{WZ}$)}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item $\text{WZ} \to \ell\nu\ell\ell$: Transforms into a formidable irreducible background whenever one of the three baseline leptons drops outside geometric acceptance or fails reconstruction, flawlessly spoofing the $2\ell + E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ target state.
    \end{itemize}
    
    \item \textbf{$\text{Z}$+Jets Backgrounds (Instrumental $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$)}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Drell--Yan + jets} ($\text{Z}/\gamma^* \to \ell\ell$ + jets): Functions as the primary reducible contaminant. While it supplies genuine leptons from a true $\text{Z}$ boson, its $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ footprint is entirely artificial, induced by severe pile-up noise or jet energy miscalculations.
    \end{itemize}
   
    \item \textbf{Fake Lepton / Instrumental Backgrounds}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{$\text{W}$ + jets}: Characterized by one genuine lepton sourced from a $\text{W}$ boson coupled with a jet falsely recognized as a secondary lepton, thereby forging a fake $2\ell$ geometry.
        \item \textbf{Fake $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$}: Massive false missing energy signatures spawned by beam halo, cosmic-ray muons, or internal hardware anomalies.
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

To precisely calculate these background rates, distinct control regions (CRs) are mapped out, with each deliberately enriched to capture a specific interfering process. A concurrent mathematical fit is then executed across the signal region and all CRs to uniformly anchor both the geometric shapes and overall yields of these backgrounds. This fitting protocol ingests event distributions to compute scaling factors, which subsequently recalibrate the MC models to align with empirical CR data. These adjusted MC outputs are then projected directly into the signal region to supply dependable background estimates, cementing the foundation for the ultimate cross-section evaluation.

The ensuing sections dissect the targeted estimation strategy engineered for each specific control region.

\subsection{Non-Resonant Background in $e^\pm\mu^\mp$ CR (With/Without $b$-Jet)}
\label{sec:bkg_emuCR}

Given the substantial operational footprint of non-resonant backgrounds, establishing highly disciplined control regions is imperative to properly constrain MC predictions. Consequently, two dedicated CRs are carved out to tightly quantify these specific yields. 

The first non-resonant CR mandates the presence of different-flavour opposite-sign (DFOS) leptons ($e^\pm\mu^\mp$). In a scenario like $\text{WW} \to \ell\nu\ell\nu$, the visible outcome can manifest as same-flavour opposite-sign (SFOS) leptons—which mimic the genuine signal—or as DFOS leptons. Because $\text{W}$ bosons decay into muons or electrons with mathematically equal probability, the expected distribution between DFOS and SFOS states naturally settles at a 1:1 ratio (incorporating both $\mu\mu$ and $ee$ SFOS combinations). Since DFOS events are easily isolated from SFOS signatures, analyzing DFOS yields provides an excellent mechanism for deducing SFOS background levels present in the Signal Region (SR). 

However, because decays from single top or top-quark pairs inherently spawn one or two $b$-jets, the resulting final state frequently harbors $b$-tagged hadronic activity. To accurately isolate these top-driven backgrounds, a secondary CR requiring exactly one $b$-jet is established. The zero $b$-jet non-resonant CR perfectly mirrors the standard non-resonant CR logic, diverging exclusively at the $b$-jet tagging parameter. Within the context of the VBS-like $\text{ZZ}jj$ study, this specific non-resonant CR indiscriminately captures both top and $\text{WW}$ background events. 

Because the ultimate objective is to project yields accurately into the SR, the foundational non-resonant background criteria strictly parallel the SR parameters, merely pivoting to filter for DFOS events. The exact parameters defining the non-resonant brackets (with and without a $b$-jet) are cataloged in Table~\ref{tab:em_definition}. Figure~\ref{fig:em-CR-prefit} illustrates the pre-fit $\Delta\phi(\vec{E}_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}, \text{Z})$ topographies across these CRs, contrasting empirical data against MC models. Within these engineered spaces, non-resonant background purity heavily eclipses 99\%.

\subsection{3-Lepton Control Region (3$\ell$ CR)}
\label{sec:bkg_3lCR}

The $\text{WZ} \to \ell\nu\ell\ell$ pathway acts as a formidable irreducible background against the $\text{ZZ} \to \ell\ell\nu\nu$ evaluation. Should one of the three component leptons fail reconstruction checks or escape detector boundaries, the resulting artificial missing transverse energy flawlessly replicates the target topology. To reliably anchor the modeling and overarching normalization of this $\text{WZ}$ interference, an inclusive 3-lepton Control Region (3$\ell$ CR) is assembled.

Additionally, a targeted transverse mass threshold ($m_{\text{T, W}} > 30~\text{GeV}$) is enforced across the third lepton and the $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$. This specific boundary is deployed aggressively to purge $\text{Z}$+jets contamination where an errant jet is falsely labeled as a lepton. By insisting on a baseline $m_{\text{T, W}}$, the filter scrubs non-prompt leptons from the pool, securing a highly pristine $\text{WZ}$ sample.

\subsection{$\text{Z}$+Jets Control Region ($\text{Z}$+Jets CR)}
\label{sec:bkg_ZjetsCR}

Accurately mapping the $\text{Z}$+jets interference presents a highly specific obstacle for the $\text{ZZ} \to \ell\ell\nu\nu$ evaluation. In stark contrast to irreducible diboson noise, the missing transverse energy witnessed in $\text{Z}$+jets collisions is entirely instrumental ("fake"). It is manufactured by detector resolution limitations, severe pile-up dynamics, and faulty jet energy calculations, rather than the existence of genuine neutrinos. As a result, the accuracy of $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ tracking within Monte Carlo grids remains heavily vulnerable to fluctuations in core detector response fidelity, jet energy corrections, and track reconstruction.

\subsubsection{Definition and Event Selection}

To reliably anchor this background using physical data, an orthogonal Control Region must be carved out that safely skirts the Signal Region while remaining heavily saturated with $\text{Z}$+jets events. This forces a strict balancing act:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Resemblance:} To accurately emulate the Signal Region's kinematic signature, the CR must probe identically elevated $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}/H_{\text{T}}$ and $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ brackets.
    \item \textbf{Purity:} To guarantee an uncontaminated background calculation, the CR must vigorously repel authentic $\text{ZZ} \to 2\ell2\nu$ signal leakage.
    \item \textbf{Orthogonality:} The applied geometric thresholds must mathematically prohibit any overlap with the Signal Region.
\end{itemize}

To navigate these constraints, the $\text{Z}$+jets CR is constructed via a precise two-dimensional geometric filter mapped across the ($E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$, $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}/H_{\text{T}}$) plane, capturing events resting immediately outside the signal perimeter. The governing logic dictates:
\begin{equation}
\small
\left( 70 < E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}} < 80~\text{GeV} \land \frac{E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}}{H_{\text{T}}} > 0.3 \right) 
\lor 
\left( E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}} > 80~\text{GeV} \land 0.3 < \frac{E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}}{H_{\text{T}}} < 0.45 \right)
\end{equation}

This formulation effectively traps the extended kinematic tail of the $\text{Z}$+jets distribution resting closest to the actual signal envelope. Figure~\ref{fig:Zjets_Control_Region} visually charts the spatial boundary segregating the $\text{Z}$+jets Control Region from the core Signal Region across this phase space.

\subsubsection{Categorization by Jet Multiplicity}

Deep analytical reviews of $\text{Z}$+jets MC models uncovered severe structural shape divergences between Monte Carlo grids and empirical data across critical variables required for unfolding. Because this artificial $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ is predominantly spawned by flawed jet calculations, the overarching event kinematics remain heavily tethered to the total hadronic jet multiplicity. 

It was quickly deduced that a solitary global scale factor could not accurately recalibrate the $\text{Z}$+jets forecast across the entire phase space. Consequently, the control region is fractured into three distinct sub-brackets dictated by jet multiplicity ($n_{\text{jets}}$):
\begin{itemize}
    \item $n_{\text{jets}} = 0$
    \item $n_{\text{jets}} = 1$
    \item $n_{\text{jets}} \ge 2$
\end{itemize}
During the simultaneous fitting procedure, autonomous scaling factors are calculated for each discrete bin. While a more granular partition was initially explored (isolating $n_{\text{jets}}=2$ from $n_{\text{jets}} \ge 3$), the mathematical scaling dynamics demonstrated sufficient uniformity at higher multiplicities to thoroughly validate a consolidated $\ge 2$ bracket.

During the final cross-section derivation, a simultaneous fit will firmly anchor the MC normalization, locking down the background leakage mapped to the signal region.
Due to severely restricted statistical volumes available inside the $\text{ZZ}jj$ phase space, the VBS-like analysis is forced to rely entirely on MC simulation to forecast the $\text{Z}$+jets interference. To safely compensate, a highly conservative normalization uncertainty is baked into the $\text{ZZ}jj$ cross-section fit.

\subsection{Minor Backgrounds}
\label{sec:bkg_minor}

A handful of secondary background processes inevitably leak into the signal region, albeit at fractionally small rates (generally $< 1\%$ of the global yield). Given their characteristically low geometric acceptance rates or inherently tiny generation cross-sections, attempting to engineer statistically viable, dedicated control regions for these events is practically impossible. Consequently, their precise footprint is quantified strictly by leveraging MC simulations locked to their theoretical production cross-sections.

These secondary contaminants encompass:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{$\text{ZZ} \to 4\ell$:} This specific interaction breaches the signal region only if a pair of constituent leptons vanishes (e.g., slipping past detector bounds or failing strict identification protocols). Although topologically akin to the genuine signal, the uncompromising requirement for massive $E_{\text{T}}^{\text{miss}}$ heavily crushes this interference.
    
    \item \textbf{Triboson Production ($\text{VVV}$):} Interactions like $\text{WZZ}$, $\text{WWZ}$, and $\text{WWW}$ feature minuscule generation cross-sections but are fully capable of yielding severe missing energy paired with dense lepton clusters.
    
    \item \textbf{$t\bar{t}\text{V}$ ($t\bar{t}\text{Z}$, $t\bar{t}\text{W}$):} The paired generation of top quarks alongside a massive vector boson. These collisions are overwhelmingly purged by the strict $b$-jet ban governing the signal region.
    
    \item \textbf{$\text{W}$ + jets:} Topologies featuring a hadronic jet falsely flagged as a secondary lepton alongside a legitimate lepton spawned from a $\text{W}$ boson. The severe lepton isolation and tracking identification protocols enforced throughout this study obliterate this background to near-zero levels.
\end{itemize}

Because physical empirical data within the simultaneous fit does not actively constrain these minor secondary backgrounds, highly conservative systematic uncertainties (generally spanning $20\%$ to $30\%$) are appended to their theoretical cross-sections to buffer against any potential modeling anomalies.
